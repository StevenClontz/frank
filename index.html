Dr. Frank Sturm

December 8, 1983 — October 14, 2014

The Department of Mathematics and Statistics of Auburn University is greatly saddened to learn of the death of Dr. Frank Sturm on October 14, 2014. Frank received his PhD in Spring 2014 under the joint advice of Professors Piotr Minc and Michel Smith, in the area of Topology. Frank was a great guy and made a lot of contributions to the department and Auburn University. He was an outstanding graduate student leader. He was liked by faculty, fellow graduate students, and his students. We will miss him greatly.

Matt Noble

We were never all that close, but I still have a Frank story to share. This happened a long time ago, so I'm probably messing up some of the details. It was finals week of (probably) December 2007. I was holding office hours in Mell Hall with some other GTAs, but there were no students in at the time so we were pretty much just shooting the breeze. Eventually the topic turned to our various plans for the Christmas break. Frank said that he was going to take a Greyhound bus back home to Houston. "Whoa," I said. "How long does that trip take?" "I'm not sure," he replied, "but one bus takes me to Mobile, and then I wait for about six hours for another bus that will take me to Houston." Hmm. My family lives in Mobile, so I told him that if he could reschedule slightly, then he could just ride down with me and time things so that he would avoid the long layover. Frank told me not to worry about it. He didn't mind the layover as it would give him time to work on some research that he had been thinking about. Most folks would have bitched endlessly about the prospect of sitting around a bus station for six hours, but Frank wasn't annoyed in the slightest. He only saw the positive in the situation. Somehow I imagine the world would be better off with more people like Frank in it.
